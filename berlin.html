INTRODUCTION BERLIN WALL JEWISH MUSEUM

BERLIN

The capital of Germany and one of its 16 states.
The second most populous city proper.
Located on the banks of Rivers Spree and Havel.
It is the centre of the Berlin-Brandenburg
Metropolitan Region with six million residents.
Around one-third of the city's area is composed
of forests, parks, gardens, rivers and lakes.

BERLIN WALL

A barrier that divided Berlin from 1961 to 1989.
Constructed by the German Democratic Republic,
Until government officials opened it in November 1989.

JEWISH MUSEUM

One of the largest Jewish Museums in Europe.
Two millennia of German-Jewish history are
on display in the permanent exhibition.
German-Jewish history is documented in the collections.
The museum was opened in 2001.
